The Baudilares

The Series follows the unfortunate events in the lives of the Baudelaire orphans.

Violet Baudilare

Violet, the eldest Baudelaire child, is an inventor. Whenever she is thinking she uses a small piece of burgundy ribbon to hold her hair back to focus. When ever she ties her hair back, she almost imeatley comes up with a great idea to get them out of what ever problem they are in.

Klaus Baudilare

Klaus, the middle child, is the researcher. He has read more books than the rest of the children combined and has a photographic memory, so he never forgets anything he reads. He is socially awkward and believes no one can be trusted until they prove themselves worthy of his trust.

Count Olaf's Associates

Count Olaf's associates are, along with Count Olaf, the main antagonists of A Series of Unfortunate Events. Many of them are conspiring to plot a way to obtain the Baudelaire Fortune, although others follow Olaf for other motivations. Many of them are on the fire-starting side of V.F.D.

Isadora Quagmire

Isadora is a poet, and is great at all types of poetry but prefers to write couplets. Her favorite poets are Lord Byron and Ogden Nash. It is likely she is the eldest triplet, but it is unknown whether or not this is true. She is often the calm and least rebelious of the triplets.

Quigley Quagmire

Quigley is a cartographer, and makes maps of everywhere he goes. He is also forward thinking and always has a plan. He is adventurous at heart but ever since his parents went missing, he is afraid of the unknown and getting lost.

White-Faced Women

Duncan is the youngest journalist in Southern California and is quite proficient in Journalism. He is a perfectionist and the most formal of the triplets. Despite this, he is very sensitive and has been depressed ever since his parents went missing.
